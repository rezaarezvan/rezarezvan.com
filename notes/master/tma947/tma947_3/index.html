Theorem: Second Order Characterization of Convexity

Let S⊆RnS \subseteq \mathbb{R}^nS⊆Rn be an open convex set and f∈C2f \in C^2f∈C2 on SSS. Then,

f is convex  ⟺  ∇2f(x)⪰0,∀x∈Sf \text{ is convex} \iff \nabla^2 f(\mathbf{x}) \succeq 0, \quad \forall \mathbf{x} \in Sf is convex⟺∇2f(x)⪰0,∀x∈S

where ∇2f(x)\nabla^2 f(\mathbf{x})∇2f(x) is the Hessian of fff at x\mathbf{x}x and ⪰0\succeq 0⪰0 means positive semidefinite. Further,

∇2f(x)≻0,∀x∈S  ⟹  f is strictly convex\nabla^2 f(\mathbf{x}) \succ 0, \quad \forall \mathbf{x} \in S \implies f \text{ is strictly convex}∇2f(x)≻0,∀x∈S⟹f is strictly convex

Notice how in the second part, we only have an implication and not an equivalence. This is because the converse is not necessarily true.
