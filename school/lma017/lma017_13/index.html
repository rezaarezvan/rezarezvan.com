Part 13 - The Jacobian

Date: 9/26/2023

Introduction

In this part we’ll cover how we change our variables into others, in the most general case.

General case of variable change

∬Df(x,y) dA\iint_D f(x, y)\ dA∬D​f(x,y) dA

We want to rewrite this in terms of uuu and vvv. Let’s say that:

x=g(u,v)y=h(u,v)x = g(u, v) \newline y = h(u, v)x=g(u,v)y=h(u,v)

We can say that we have a transformation, TTT, from the uvuvuv-plane to the xyxyxy-plane, given by our functions ggg and hhh.

We need to make some assumptions of TTT to make our lives easier.

  1. TTT is a C1C^1C1-transformation, meaning that ggg and hhh have continuous partial derivatives.

  2. TTT is an injective transformation (meaning it is 1-to-1). This means that we can express uuu and vvv in terms of xxx and yyy.

With this, we can define the Jacobian matrix.

Definition

The jacobian matrix of a transformation, TTT, is:

∂(x,y)∂(u,v)=det[∂x∂u∂x∂v∂y∂u∂y∂v]\dfrac{\partial(x, y)}{\partial(u, v)} = det \begin{bmatrix} \dfrac{\partial x}{\partial u} & \dfrac{\partial x}{\partial v} \newline \dfrac{\partial y}{\partial u} & \dfrac{\partial y}{\partial v} \end{bmatrix}∂(u,v)∂(x,y)​=det​∂u∂x​∂u∂y​​∂v∂x​∂v∂y​​​

Suppose TTT is a transformation, from the uvuvuv-plane to the xyxyxy-plane. Assuming that the jacobian for TTT, is non-zero, then:

∬Df(x,y) dA=∬Sf(x(u,v),y(u,v))∣∂(x,y)∂(u,v)∣ dA\iint_D f(x, y)\ dA = \iint_S f(x(u, v), y(u, v)) \vert \tfrac{\partial(x, y)}{\partial(u, v)} \vert \ dA∬D​f(x,y) dA=∬S​f(x(u,v),y(u,v))∣∂(u,v)∂(x,y)​∣ dA

Some applications of multiple integrals

Let’s see what we can achieve with multiple integrals.

Computing mass of solid

Let EEE be an arbitrary solid, with a density function, ρ(x,y,z)\rho(x, y, z)ρ(x,y,z)

We say that the mass of the solid is:

∭Eρ(x,y,z) dV\iiint_E \rho(x, y, z)\ dV∭E​ρ(x,y,z) dV

We perform the same proof as for integrals for solids, but with the density function instead. Taking the sum and limit of small points and their densities.

Center of Mass

One very important application is finding the center of mass of a solid.

Given a physical system with mim_imi​ points, the center of mass has the coordinates, (x0,y0,z0)(x_0, y_0, z_0)(x0​,y0​,z0​).

Therefore:

x0=∑mixi∑mix_0 = \dfrac{\sum m_i x_i}{\sum m_i}x0​=∑mi​∑mi​xi​​ y0=∑miyi∑miy_0 = \dfrac{\sum m_i y_i}{\sum m_i}y0​=∑mi​∑mi​yi​​ z0=∑mizi∑miz_0 = \dfrac{\sum m_i z_i}{\sum m_i}z0​=∑mi​∑mi​zi​​
